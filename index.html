ERVQ: Enhancing Residual Vector Quantization in Audio Codecs through Intra- and Inter-Codebook Optimization



Anonymous Author(s).


In this paper, we present ERVQ, Enhancing Residual Vector Quantization, a novel enhancement strategy for residual vector quantization (RVQ) framework in audio codecs, aiming to overcome the codebook collapse issue and improve overall codec performance. ERVQ employs both intra- and inter-codebook optimization techniques. The intra-codebook optimization introduces an online clustering strategy and a code balancing loss to ensure balanced and effective codebook utilization. The inter-codebook optimization enhances the diversity of quantized features by minimizing the structural similarity between successive quantizations. Implemented with minimal code changes, ERVQ can be integrated into various audio codecs utilizing RVQ or its variants. Our experiments demonstrate that ERVQ significantly improves audio codec performance across different models, sampling rates, and bitrates, achieving superior quality and generalization capabilities. Further experiments indicate that audio codecs improved by the ERVQ strategy can also boost unified audio-and-text large language models (LLMs).
